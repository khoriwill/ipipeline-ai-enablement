# 90-Day AI Enablement Roadmap — iPipeline

**Role:** AI Enablement Lead (Program Manager, AI Transformation)  
**Objective:** Move from AI investment to measurable AI adoption across internal business functions

---

## The Philosophy

The first 90 days are not about building everything. They are about building the right thing, proving it works, and creating the foundation that everything else grows from.

Speed without foundation = shelfware. Foundation without speed = lost momentum.

The goal is one successful, well-measured, well-communicated win by day 90 — and a clear roadmap for what comes next.

---

## Phase 1: Discover (Days 1–30)

**The goal: understand before you build.**

### Week 1–2: Listen and Map
- Meet with leaders across Professional Services, Customer Support, Sales Ops, and HR
- Ask one question in every meeting: *"Where does your team spend time on work that feels repetitive or frustrating?"*
- Document every workflow mentioned — do not filter yet
- Review existing Microsoft 365 usage: what tools are already deployed and actually being used?
- Audit current AI tool landscape: what do employees already use, officially or unofficially?

### Week 3–4: Prioritize
- Cluster workflows by: frequency, time cost, AI suitability, and team readiness
- Identify the **highest-impact, lowest-risk** use case for the first agent
- Confirm with leadership: Professional Services is the right starting point
- Establish the AI Center of Excellence working group (AI Lead + IT + Legal + PS Lead + CS Lead)
- Define success metrics and capture baseline data before building anything

**Deliverables by Day 30:**
- [ ] Workflow opportunity map (all departments)
- [ ] Use case prioritization matrix
- [ ] Baseline metrics captured for PS team
- [ ] CoE working group established with meeting cadence
- [ ] Executive briefing: "Here's what we found, here's where we're starting, here's why"

---

## Phase 2: Pilot (Days 31–60)

**The goal: build one thing, prove it works, create believers.**

### Week 5–6: Build the PS Workflow Assistant
- Configure Copilot Studio agent in the Microsoft 365 tenant
- Connect SharePoint knowledge base (SOPs, checklists, FAQs)
- Build and test 4 core conversation topics (onboarding, ticket summary, escalation, FAQ)
- Internal testing with PS Lead — find the gaps before users do
- IT security review and sign-off

### Week 7–8: Soft Launch
- Deploy to 3–5 PS reps (early adopters, not resistors)
- Run 30-minute onboarding session: "Here's what it does, here's what it doesn't do, here's how to give feedback"
- Weekly check-in with pilot users — listen more than you talk
- Fix issues fast — pilot users are watching to see if you're responsive
- Start collecting efficiency data

**Deliverables by Day 60:**
- [ ] Agent live in Teams for pilot group
- [ ] Week 2, 4 efficiency surveys completed
- [ ] First round of topic corrections based on user feedback
- [ ] 30-day report delivered to PS leadership
- [ ] Decision: ready for full PS team rollout?

---

## Phase 3: Scale (Days 61–90)

**The goal: expand what works, document what we learned, plan what's next.**

### Week 9–10: Full PS Rollout
- Expand agent to full PS team (10–15 reps)
- Lunch-and-learn session: peer-to-peer demo from pilot reps (not from you — from them)
- Update FAQ and knowledge base based on pilot learnings
- Establish ongoing feedback channel in Teams

### Week 11–12: Measure and Plan
- 90-day metrics review: hours saved, adoption rate, escalation trends, ROI estimate
- Identify next team for Phase 2 rollout (Customer Support is the likely next)
- Document lessons learned from PS pilot
- Build Phase 2 roadmap: what we're building, for whom, and when
- Executive presentation: program results, ROI, Phase 2 ask

**Deliverables by Day 90:**
- [ ] Full PS team on agent
- [ ] 90-day ROI report (quantified)
- [ ] Customer Support use case scoped and approved
- [ ] Phase 2 roadmap presented to leadership
- [ ] Governance framework reviewed and updated

## What Could Go Wrong (and How to Handle It)

**"People aren't using it"**  
→ Adoption problem, not technology problem. Go back to the team. Find out why. Is it where they work? Is the training insufficient? Is there a trust issue? Fix the cause, not the symptom.

**"The agent gives wrong answers"**  
→ Knowledge base problem. Update the source document. Retrain the topic. Communicate transparently with users. Speed of response to issues determines long-term trust.

**"Leadership wants it in every department immediately"**  
→ Hold the line on the phased approach. One well-executed pilot is worth more than five simultaneous half-baked rollouts. Show them the 90-day ROI and use it to fund the right pace of expansion.

**"IT is slowing everything down"**  
→ Involve IT from day one, not after you've built something. The working group structure in Phase 1 exists specifically to prevent this. IT partnership is a feature, not a blocker.

---

## The Measure of Success at Day 90

At the end of 90 days, the question is not "did we deploy an agent?" The question is:

1. Do PS reps actually use it every day?
2. Can we quantify the time it saved?
3. Do users trust it?
4. Does leadership see the value and want to expand it?
5. Do we know what we'd do differently next time?

If the answer to all five is yes — the program is working.
